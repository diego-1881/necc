Our Story

Every child with autism deserves a full, productive, and rewarding life. At The New England Center for Children® (NECC®), we provide comprehensive services for children with autism: through day and residential programs, partner classrooms in public schools, consulting services, and autism curriculum software for teachers.

To maximize our impact, we focus on preparing teachers and behavior analysts through training, professional development, and graduate program opportunities. In fact, NECC has trained thousands of professionals who are now providing state-of-the-art care and services worldwide, whether through consulting, teaching, or their own programs.

The John and Diane Kim Autism Institute is home to our research center, where we study how children with autism best learn. This data is published in top peer-reviewed journals and is also directed into the ACE® ABA Software System, our autism curriculum used by educators around the world.

Mission
& Vision

Our Mission is to create a global network of educators, researchers, and programs so that those living with autism may live fuller lives.

Our Vision is a world where autism is not a barrier to happiness, growth, and independence.

1975

Vinnie Strully, Dudley Orr, and John Pangburn found the Efficacy Research Institute (ERI) on the grounds of the Taunton State Hospital. The school starts with six students. Kathy Foster is hired.

1979

ERI expands to serve 20 students. The need for effective autism education programs grows.

1980

Vinnie Strully is featured in Parade Magazine.

ERI takes over Spear Education Center in Framingham. ERI in Taunton becomes ERIT, managed by Kathy Foster. Spear Education Center becomes ERIF and is managed by Vinnie Strully.

1986

ERIT and ERIF merge to become The New England Center for Autism (NECA) and move to our present location in Southborough, MA.

1992

The master’s in applied behavior analysis (ABA) program through Northeastern University is established.

NECC partners with Simmons College to provide a master’s degree in education and licensure in severe special needs.

1998

NECC receives The Doug Flutie, Jr. Foundation for Autism, Inc. grant for the development of the Autism Curriculum Encyclopedia® (ACE).

2000

NECC receives the National Award for Model Professional Development from the U.S. Department of Education.

The organization’s 25th Anniversary celebration is held at Boston’s World Trade Center.

2003

The first lesson is published in the ACE®.

2007

NECC opens a school in Abu Dhabi, as a directive of His Highness Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, Crown Prince of Abu Dhabi.

Vinnie Strully wins the Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year Award.

2009

The ACE® software reaches 1,000 lessons spanning three lesson formats (Discrete Trial, Task Analysis and Incidental Teaching).

Vinnie Strully receives an honorary Doctor of Humane Letters from Western New England University.

The ACE® software system makes its way to New York City, which launches a pilot program to integrate the ACE® into public school classrooms.

2012

The Michael F. Downey Center for Childhood Development opens to provide care for children of staff. Michael Downey was the financial architect of NECC and served 36 years, 16 as CFO. His work ensured that NECC could grow to become what it is today.

2016

The John and Diane Kim Autism Institute opens in October, marking the biggest expansion of the NECC campus to date. The Kim Autism Institute is home to 33,00 square-feet of office, graduate classroom, and research space.

2021

ACE® surpasses 10,000 learners worldwide.

The COVID workforce shortage impacts all of Massachusetts. NECC consolidates programs and accelerates international recruiting efforts.

NECC establishes a Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion office.

2023

NECC and Vinnie Strully are honored at Autism Speaks Gala in NYC. Smigel Supersmile® Dental Clinic opens at NECC.

Dr. Jessica Sassi, BCBA-D, LABA, succeeds Vinnie as CEO. Vinnie transitions to the role of Founder and Executive Advisor.

NECC opens new clinics in Abu Dhabi and Dubai.
